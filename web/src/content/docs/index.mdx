Small teams can't run ITIL — it assumes a department and a budget for process nobody
has. But they still have to decide what's worth building, design it so it can actually
be run, and avoid the 2&nbsp;a.m. page for something nobody wrote down. FitSD is the
smallest amount of that discipline a handful of people can keep up.

## Two things do most of the work

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="One front door" icon="seti:lock">
    New work enters through a single intake and two light decision gates — **Gate&nbsp;1**
    (*is this worth doing?*) and **Gate&nbsp;2** (*is it ready to build?*). Effort is
    earned at a gate; nothing is built on a whim.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Readiness is the finish line" icon="approve-check">
    Nothing reaches live service until it is *evidenced* as ready — documented,
    recoverable (with a tested restore), secure, monitored and supportable. “Done”
    means operable, not just switched on.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Who's it for?

FitSD pays off differently depending on where you sit — pick your side.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Governance & leadership" icon="approve-check">
    **Fund the right work — and prove it's run well — without a PMO.**

    - **Decisions on a page, not in a corridor.** Proposals arrive in your language — driver, cost of doing nothing, options, effort — with a clear yes/no.
    - **An audit trail without an audit team.** Acceptance records, a risk register and change records — the evidence customers and regulators ask for, produced as you work.
    - **Risk controlled at the front door.** Nothing's built on a whim; new tools and suppliers arrive through due diligence.
    - **A real on-ramp to ISO 27001 & NIS2.** Lightweight now; the discipline maps onto the heavy standards when you need them.
    - **“Done” means operable.** Fewer 2&nbsp;a.m. incidents and unsupportable services — lower operational risk and run-cost.

    [Executive summary](/framework/pitch-and-summary/) · [How it maps to ISO 27001 / NIS2](/reference/standards-alignment/)
  </Card>
  <Card title="Engineers & implementers" icon="rocket">
    **Get heard, ship it once, and never carry it alone.**

    - **Get your idea funded.** The Idea Brief and Gate&nbsp;1 frame your case in leadership's language — good ideas stop dying for want of a pitch.
    - **Just enough process.** Two light gates and a done-check — not ITIL. The smallest discipline a handful of people can keep up.
    - **Keep your own stack and workflow.** FitSD says *what* good looks like; you keep the *how* — it hands the build back to the way you already ship.
    - **Design operability in, up front.** Gate&nbsp;2 makes you say how you'll back it up, monitor and support it — you find the gap now, not in six months.
    - **“Done” means you're not on the hook at 2&nbsp;a.m.** Documented, recoverable (tested restore), monitored and supportable before go-live.

    [Quickstart](/quickstart/) · [Fill in the forms](/forms/)
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## How it stays light

FitSD owns only a thin set of testable requirements. It ships a full, ready-to-use
process for one capability — **Solution Development**, the front door — and for the
other four it states the requirement and points at the standard or process a team
already runs. That single choice is how it can require change, incident and security
discipline *without rebuilding those disciplines*.
